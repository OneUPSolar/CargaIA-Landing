# CargaIA Founder Brief + Strategy Pack

## Working Title
**CargaIA**  
*A safety-first EV charging infrastructure and subscription platform for Mexico and cross-border markets.*

---

# 1. Executive Summary

CargaIA is a technology-enabled EV charging and energy infrastructure platform designed to solve a major and growing problem: unsafe, unpermitted, and poorly designed charger installations in homes and businesses, especially in Mexico.

The company’s core opportunity is not just selling chargers. Its real value lies in becoming the trusted infrastructure layer between EV buyers, property owners, installers, utilities, equipment suppliers, financing partners, and distributed energy systems.

CargaIA aims to make electric vehicle adoption safer, easier, and more economically viable by offering:

- Code-conscious installation pathways
- Subscription-based charger and electrical infrastructure deployment
- Automated design, permitting, and utility-submission workflows
- Networked installer oversight and inspection verification
- Optional integration with solar, batteries, smart meters, and controlled charging
- Residential and commercial charging monetization models

The initial market focus is **Tijuana and major border cities**, with expansion across Mexico and selective U.S. border-state deployment.

---

# 2. Core Problem

## The Market Failure
EV adoption is accelerating faster than the supporting electrical infrastructure.

In Mexico, many customers are buying EVs without:
- proper charger planning
- safe electrical upgrades
- permitted installations
- utility coordination
- demand/load management
- integration with solar or backup energy systems

This creates real-world damage and risk:
- melted meter cans
- damaged electrical panels
- overloaded circuits
- charger underperformance
- unsafe unlicensed work
- poor customer experience
- strain on utility infrastructure

## Why This Matters
A bad charging install is not just inconvenient. It can:
- damage the property
- damage utility equipment
- shorten equipment life
- create liability exposure
- undermine EV adoption itself

CargaIA exists to eliminate that chaos.

CargaIA’s mission is to create a safe, compliant, scalable transition from gasoline-powered mobility to electrified transport by deploying managed EV charging infrastructure tied to real electrical readiness.

# 6. Business Model

## Primary Revenue Model
### Subscription
Customers pay a monthly fee for a managed charging and electrical infrastructure package.

This may include:
- charger hardware
- installation
- system activation
- service/support
- monitoring
- maintenance
- optional solar integration
- smart-meter or load-management functionality

Early customers may:

For customers who do not want long-term monthly commitments:
- offer a prepaid service agreement

# 9. Operational Model

## Residential Flow
1. Customer hears about CargaIA through ads, partnerships, dealerships, or referrals
2. Customer lands on the website or waitlist page
3. Customer watches a short explainer and submits interest
4. CargaIA qualifies the property/use case
5. System design and scope are created
6. Required electrical upgrades, metering, and charger package are defined
7. Plan set / compliance package is generated
8. Installer is assigned through the network
9. Installation is completed
10. Inspection is verified through app-based photos, videos, and functionality checks
11. Installer gets paid after approval
12. Customer begins subscription or prepaid service term

## Commercial Flow
1. CargaIA identifies a viable site
2. Host or site owner is qualified
3. Revenue model is structured
4. Charging layout and infrastructure scope are engineered
5. Capital deployment path is defined
6. Install and commissioning are completed
7. Charging is activated and monitored
8. Revenue, maintenance, and usage are managed through the platform

# 12. Objections and Counters

## Objection 1
“I do not want to be tied to a 5-year agreement.”

### Counter
- offer prepaid option
- offer buyout structure
- offer finance alternative
- frame service as infrastructure security, not just equipment rental

## Objection 2
“I can just hire my own electrician.”

### Counter
- most electricians are not designing full EV energy ecosystems
- code, utility, metering, and safety requirements are being missed
- failed installs are more expensive than doing it right once

## Objection 3
“This sounds expensive.”

### Counter
- compare against:
  - utility damage
  - failed installs
  - noncompliance
  - delayed charging
  - commercial opportunity loss
- frame subscription around convenience, readiness, and protection

## Objection 4
“I’m not ready to buy an EV yet.”

### Counter
- position CargaIA as EV readiness
- educate first
- create a waitlist and learning funnel
- let them reserve future access
